What We Do

We at HVAC Optimization Solutions specialize in efficiency. By integrating a system of sensors in buildings’ ductwork, we can maximize HVAC system performance, minimize cost of operation, and verify if the system is performing within legal code.

The American Society of Heating, Refrigeration, and Air Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE) standards have specifications regarding ventilation for facilities depending on the purpose of the room. Often, facilities’ air ducts have leaks and thus the theoretical performance of the ventilation system of the building is not the reality. As a result, these buildings do not meet standards for air ventilation. Additionally, businesses rarely have ventilation systems operating at maximum efficiency and are thus losing money. Leaks in ventilation systems often exist throughout the entire building adding up to much more significant leakage than is often assumed by the building owners.

By deploying air velocity sensors in ductwork, we are able to identify inefficiencies in the system based on velocity changes through the ducts. With these sensors, we can calculate the the cubic feet of air per minute (CFM) moving through ducts. Based on the changes in CFM between floors or rooms, along with pressure differential, we are able to identify specific inconsistencies that deviate from ideal behavior. We can analyze these flaws, and pinpoint the location of inefficiencies. Based on the size of these leaks, clogs, and poor original designs, we can propose solutions to fix these problems.
